Wallababy Kangaroo

A wallaby is a small or middle-sized macropod native to Australia and New Guinea, with introduced populations in New Zealand, Hawaii, the United Kingdom and other countries.

  • Scientific Name: Macropodidae
  • Average Length: 45 to 105 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: Wallabies are widely distributed across Australia. They also can be found on the island of New Guinea.

Wallabies are herbivores whose diet consists of a wide range of grasses, vegetables, leaves and other foliage. They also have a powerful tail that is used mostly for balance and support.
